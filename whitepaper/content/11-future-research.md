# 10. Future Research

Cineora is a working protocol and a research program. Four threads are open and are funded by the protocol's R&D budget, not by speculative grants.

## 10.1 Cross-Reality Portals

A **Cross-Reality Portal** is a render surface that consumes the same world state but projects it through a different sensory channel: a browser tab, a headset, a spatial display, a holographic plate. The research problem is **viewport-independent state**: a scene must remain canonically identical across radically different presentation media. Active work focuses on lossy semantic compression of scene graphs that preserve narrative meaning under aggressive bandwidth constraints, and on the perceptual equivalence tests that establish when two portals are showing the "same" world.

## 10.2 Neural Narrative Interfaces

Direct neural input — electromyography, electroencephalography, and emerging non-invasive BCI devices — promises a future in which audience participation moves from click to intent. The protocol commits in advance to a **consent-first architecture** for any such interface: nothing is recorded without explicit, revocable, per-session consent; nothing is written on-chain without cryptographic separation between identity and physiology; and the agent mesh's bounded-autonomy rules are extended to treat any neural signal as a low-trust input regardless of source.

## 10.3 Civilization Analytics

A civilization produces data about itself: which scenes are remembered, which characters persist, which dialogues become folklore. The research program investigates **privacy-preserving aggregate metrics** — differential privacy, secure multi-party computation, and zero-knowledge rollups — that allow a world to understand its own culture without surveilling its individual members. Open questions include the appropriate epsilon budget for cultural research, the governance threshold required to unlock raw versus aggregated views, and the audit trail that makes metric definitions themselves contestable.

## 10.4 Story Inheritance

When a creator is gone, what happens to their world? **Story Inheritance** is the research thread that designs the legal, cryptographic, and economic mechanisms by which a civilization survives its founder. Open questions include: how dormant is dormant (the threshold at which a Legacy Constitution activates its succession rules); how does a civilization recognize a new steward (cryptographic proof of relationship, social proof, or both); how is canonical drift across generations bounded without freezing the work; and how are derivative worlds credited to a lineage. The first prototypes ship in Phase 4 and are evaluated over multi-year observation windows rather than launch-day metrics.

These are not promises. They are commitments to investigate, in public, with results published whether they validate or invalidate the underlying hypothesis.
