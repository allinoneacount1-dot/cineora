# Abstract — The Living Cinematic Civilization

Cineora is a decentralized protocol that converts cinematic imagination into persistent, ownable, and economically alive digital civilizations. It is not a video generator, a token launchpad, a creator tool, or a social platform. It is a new substrate — a layer beneath the production, distribution, and monetization of narrative worlds — where stories behave less like files and more like organisms.

The protocol emerges from a single observation: generative AI has made cinematic content effectively infinite. OpenAI has given the world intelligence at scale; Midjourney has democratized imagination; Solana has reduced the cost of digital ownership by orders of magnitude. What none of them have solved is **permanence**. Every minute, millions of synthetic scenes are rendered, watched once, and forgotten. Abundance without continuity produces noise, not culture.

Cineora answers this with four primitives:

1. **Living Scene NFTs** — on-chain scenes that carry state, evolve via governance, and accrue cultural memory rather than merely trading as collectibles.
2. **An Agent Mesh** — sixteen specialized AI agents (Director, Screenwriter, Cinematographer, Composer, Lore, and others) that collaborate inside a world, each with bounded autonomy, auditable memory, and an economic identity.
3. **Seven Civilization Layers** — Aurora, Genesis, Memory, Legacy, Cinema, Governance, and Evolution — that together define how a world is rendered, born, remembered, owned, watched, ruled, and upgraded.
4. **A Token Economy ($CINE)** — a fixed-supply asset that funds the treasury, rewards contributors, recycles revenue through burns and buybacks, and is designed to remain functional across 50-year horizons rather than quarterly cycles.

The north star is explicit: *become the cinematic operating system of the internet* — not by competing with studios, but by providing the substrate on which the next century of narrative civilization can be built, inherited, and preserved.
